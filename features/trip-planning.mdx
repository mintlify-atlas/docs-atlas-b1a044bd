TREK's planner organizes your trip into days, lets you search for places, drag them into your schedule, and export optimized routes — all in one view.

## Creating a trip

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the trips dashboard">
    From the home screen, click **New Trip**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in trip details">
    Provide a title, optional description, start and end dates, and the trip's default currency. You can also upload a cover image (JPG, PNG, GIF, or WebP, up to 20 MB).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    TREK generates one day per calendar date between your start and end dates. Trips without dates default to 7 blank days. The maximum trip length is **365 days**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can update the title, dates, currency, and cover image at any time from the trip settings panel.
</Note>

## Drag-and-drop planner

The planner displays each day as a column. Places you've added to the trip appear in the sidebar and can be dragged into any day.

- **Reorder within a day** — drag a place card up or down to change visit order.
- **Move between days** — drag a place card from one day column to another.
- **Unassign** — drag a place back to the unscheduled sidebar.

## Place search

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="OpenStreetMap (free)" icon="map">
    Searches via Nominatim. No API key required. Returns name, address, and coordinates. Available on all TREK instances out of the box.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Google Places" icon="star">
    Returns photos, star ratings, opening hours, phone numbers, and website links. Requires a Google Places API (New) key configured in the Admin Panel.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

TREK automatically uses Google Places when a key is configured and falls back to OpenStreetMap otherwise.

### Assigning places to days

After adding a place to your trip you can:

- Drag it into a specific day column in the planner.
- Set a **start time** and **end time** directly on the place card.

## Day notes

Each day supports timestamped notes with selectable icons. Notes can be reordered by dragging, making them useful for quick reminders, meeting points, or reservation details that don't belong to a specific place.

## Day title naming

Click the day header in the planner to rename it (for example, "Travel day" or "Free afternoon"). The date is always shown alongside the custom title.

## Route optimization

TREK can reorder the places in a day to minimize travel time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a day">
    Select a day in the planner that has at least two places assigned.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimize">
    Click **Optimize route**. TREK reorders the places using a nearest-neighbor algorithm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export to Google Maps">
    Click **Open in Google Maps** to open the optimized route in Google Maps with all stops pre-loaded as waypoints.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reservations

The Reservations panel tracks bookings associated with your trip.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Supported reservation types">
    - **Flight** — airline, flight number, departure and arrival airports
    - **Hotel** — check-in/check-out dates, linked accommodation spanning multiple days
    - **Restaurant** — dining reservation with time and location
    - **Train** — rail bookings with departure and arrival details
    - **Car** — car rental pick-up and drop-off
    - **Cruise** — cruise booking with port details
    - **Event** — concerts, shows, and ticketed events
    - **Tour** — guided tour or excursion
    - **Activity** — adventures, sports, and experiences
    - **Other** — any booking that doesn't fit the above categories
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What you can record">
    Each reservation stores a title, date and time, location, confirmation number, notes, and status (pending, confirmed, cancelled). You can attach files such as e-tickets directly to a reservation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Linking to days and places">
    Reservations can be linked to a specific day and to a place already in your trip, so they appear in context in the planner view.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Hotel reservations can automatically create an accommodation record that spans multiple days, showing where you're staying on the day view.
</Tip>
