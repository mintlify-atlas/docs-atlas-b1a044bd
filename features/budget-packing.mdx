## Budget tracking

The Budget panel lets you record and categorize all trip expenses, split costs across travelers, and visualize spending with a pie chart.

### Adding expenses

Each budget item has:

- **Name** — a description of the expense.
- **Category** — used to group items in the chart (e.g., Accommodation, Food, Transport).
- **Total price** — the full cost of the item.
- **Persons** — how many people share this expense. You can assign specific trip members instead of entering a number manually.
- **Days** — optional number of days to factor into a per-day cost (e.g., a 5-night hotel stay).
- **Date** — the date the expense occurred.
- **Note** — any additional details.

### Splitting costs

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Per person" icon="users">
    Assign specific trip members to an item. TREK divides the total evenly and tracks who has paid their share.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Per day" icon="calendar">
    Enter the number of days to calculate a daily rate alongside the total cost.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

To mark a member's share as paid, open the budget item and toggle their paid status.

### Settlement

TREK calculates who owes whom based on paid statuses and shows an optimized set of transfers to settle all balances — so the group needs to make as few payments as possible.

### Pie chart visualization

The budget overview displays a pie chart breaking down spending by category, giving you a quick picture of where money is going.

### Trip currency

The trip's default currency is set when creating the trip and can be changed in trip settings. Each budget item can also use a different currency if needed — useful when recording expenses in local currencies while tracking the overall budget in your home currency.

<Note>
  TREK does not perform automatic currency conversion. Exchange rates are not applied; currency codes are recorded for reference only.
</Note>

## Packing lists

The Packing panel helps you build category-based checklists, assign items to travelers, and track packing progress across the group.

### Adding items

Items belong to a **category** (e.g., Clothing, Toiletries, Electronics). You can add items one at a time or bulk-import them. Checking an item marks it as packed and updates the progress indicator.

### Assigning categories to members

Each category can be assigned to one or more trip members, making it clear who is responsible for packing those items. Assigned members receive a notification.

### Progress tracking

The packing panel shows overall progress as a percentage of checked items, and breaks down progress per category so you can see at a glance what's still missing.

### Packing templates

Templates let you save a standard packing list and apply it to any trip with one click.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a template">
    Go to **Admin Panel → Packing Templates**. Create a template with categories and items (e.g., a "Beach Trip" template with Swimwear, Sunscreen, Snorkeling gear).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply to a trip">
    Open the Packing panel on any trip and click **Apply template**. Select the template to add all its items to the trip's packing list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Templates are managed by admins and are available to all users on the instance. They're a good way to share standard lists for common trip types.
</Tip>

### Bag tracking

Bag tracking is an optional feature that can be enabled or disabled by an admin.

When enabled, you can:

- Create named bags (e.g., Carry-on, Checked bag, Backpack) with custom colors.
- Assign individual packing items to a bag.
- Record item weights in grams.
- Set a weight limit per bag to track total weight distribution.

<Note>
  Bag tracking is toggled in the **Admin Panel → Settings**. When disabled, the bag column is hidden from the packing list.
</Note>
